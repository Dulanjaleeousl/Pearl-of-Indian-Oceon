Kegalle

Kegalle is a large town in Sabaragamuwa Province of Sri Lanka. It is located on the Colombo‐Kandy road, approximately 78 km from Colombo and 40 km from Kandy. It is the main town in the Kegalle District, which is one of two districts which comprise Sabaragamuwa Province. The town is governed by an Urban Council. The surrounding area produces graphite, precious stones and agricultural products such as rice. Kegalle has an agricultural based economy and mainly produces rubber. Kegalle is the nearest town to the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, a major tourist attraction in the area, which is 10.5 km to the north-east.

1. Millennium Elephant Foundation Kegalle

Millennium Elephant Foundation is an organization and charity set up to rescue and care for captive Asian elephants in Sri Lanka. The foundation is situated on a 15 acre estate by the name of Samaragiri, which is located 10 km northwest of Kegalle, within the Sabaragamuwa Province of Sri Lanka. As of 2014 there are 9 elephants that either permanently or temporarily reside at the sanctuary. These elephants are taken care of by 13 local mahouts and a number of foreign volunteers.The foundation is funded predominantly by tourists and volunteer fees. This money is used to develop and improve conditions for the elephants at MEF, as well as run an education program for farmers and their families in Habarana.

2. Kurulu Kele

“Kurulu Kele” is situated in the Golahela Grama Seva Division of Kegalle District. The route to the venue is pretty straightforward for someone travelling from Colombo. Be it public transport or your own ride, firstly one has to arrive at Kegalle Town via the Colombo-Kandy Main Road and from there you have to take the Bandaranayake Mawatha. From March to July and again in October and November, the monsoonal rains are said to be active. If you choose to visit “Kurulu Kele” during these times, chances are that you may not be able to enjoy the scenic beauty of the birds completely. Therefore, for your optimum satisfaction a journey to “Kurulu Kele” should be ideally arranged in August, September, December, January or February.
